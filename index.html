I am currently a final year PhD student at the University of Auckland School of Computer Science, New Zealand. My research focus is on machine learning for online (streaming) systems, specifically, investigating how we can adapt machine learning algorithms to changing conditions faced in the real world.

The internet is allowing a growing number of streaming datasets, where new data is collected and added in real time. For example, one government agency I have worked with collects air quality data in real-time from a network of sensors. Other examples include nearly all online businesses which collect information. Traditional machine learning methods are designed around static datasets, making the assumtion that data will stay similar over time. If data changes, for example, shifting weather patterns or consumer behaviour, these static methods can become disastrously out of date.

My research aims to allow machine learning methods to detect changes, identify the new emerging patterns, and adapt themselves to retain performance. In particular, I am interested in identifying recurring patterns, which can be reused for performance benefits, and also tracked over time to trace changes in hidden context.
